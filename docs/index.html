Video-to-Video Face Re-Aging with Delta-Propagated Temporal Consistency

Anonymous Authors

DeltaConsistency enables flexible prompt-based face re-aging of any real-world video or CGI to younger (de-aged) or older (aged) direction with high fidelity and temporal coherence. In contrast to conventional StyleGAN-based approaches, it is built on diffusion model, a variety of input (here, side face (left), cartoon-style (center), computer-generated images (right)) can be seamlessly de-/aged.

Abstract

Face re-aging has garnered popularity in computer graphics applications due to its cost efficiency. For practical use, it is imperative to edit faces in a given real-video with temporal consistency without color flickering or geometric distortion. However, an approach to temporally consistent face re-aging is barely explored, lagging behind the those of image-level one. In this paper, we therefore tackle this issue by proposing video face re-aging diffusion models, coined \textit{DeltaConsistency} which is capable of temporally consistent editing with high-fidelity within real-video. Unlike existing face re-aging networks which generate entire pixels in image, we focus on age-related visual margin, delta. Since this delta brings stability for facial re-aging visual effects, our system ensure the temporal coherence without separate fine-tuning or additional condition like facial mask. Moreover, prompt-driven local editing is adopted under diffusion model, our method takes advantage of flexible editing. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate the superiority of our proposed method with improved re-aging results on real-world video editing and out-of-domain video, significantly surpassing existing video editing models. Our framework achieve a state-of-the-art in video face re-aging.

Conceptual behavior of delta operation
